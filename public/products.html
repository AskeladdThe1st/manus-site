Ready-to-ship agents with clear roles, plus room for new specialties.

Tutor agents, interview agents, and a growing catalog run on the same framework so new roles launch quickly and stay governable. This page goes deeper into use cases, fit, and limits.

Overview of available agents

Tutor Agents

Structured, subject-specific learning companions. They run scoped drills, track progress, and deliver measurable learning at scale.

Use when teams need repeatable learning, assessments, and reinforcement—not open-ended Q&A.

Interview Agents

Conduct structured interviews with constraints, role-specific checklists, and scoring logic to standardize evaluations.

Use when consistency, audit trails, and bias reduction are critical to decisions.

Future Specialized Agents

A growing catalog: onboarding coaches, QA reviewers, operations copilots. Built on the same framework for quick, governable launches.

Use when a narrow role needs repeatable decisions with clear guardrails.

When to use which agent

  • Tutor Agents: when teams need repeatable learning with progress tracking and scoped drills.
  • Interview Agents: when decisions depend on consistent questioning, scoring, and auditability.
  • Future Specialized Agents: when a focused role is needed for onboarding, QA, or operations support.

How they fit

Each agent is defined by a role, a boundary, and an evaluation loop. New roles inherit the same guardrails.

Role What the agent owns
Boundary What stays out of scope
Evaluation How outcomes are scored
Escalation When humans step in

Use cases and limits

Tutor Agents — good for

Skill ramp, onboarding cohorts, policy refreshers.

Limitations

Not for unbounded brainstorming or replacing instructors; keep scope narrow.

Interview Agents — good for

Discovery calls, audits, qualitative research with consistent prompts.

Limitations

Not for final hiring decisions; humans own judgment and escalation.

Future agents — good for

Process QA, policy compliance checks, routine operational reviews.

Limitations

Avoid ambiguous authority; boundaries and approvals remain explicit.
